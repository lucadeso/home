☺ About me ☺

How about doing a rewind? Do you mind? No? Perfect!

So nice to meet you again, my name is Luca Desogus (yes, I'm Italian although my Sardinian surname can mislead you).
I study — or at least I'm trying to — Communication Design at Politecnico di Milano
and actually I'm quite fond of typography, illustration, 3d rendering and motion graphics.

Even though I'm only 21, in all these years I built myself from the ground up and I'll go on studying graphic design
because, you know, you learn something new everyday, don't you? So, having said that,
how about we take a look at some of my work?

Oh, before I forget: I'm the guy in these pics below — just in case you didn't get it!

First Things First

The name of this booklet is a reference to a manifesto written and published by Ken Garland along with other designers in 1964. During Typography Course hosted by Marta Bernstein,
I had to design a layout for this using only one font and black color.
Iso Rivolta Brand Magazine

This is magazine designed for Iso Rivolta, an Italian brand no longer alive, in Lab of Metadesign. My team and I had to express the main value of the brand, enterprise. Our declination was focused on challenge, as you can see by the name of the magazine.
Aptive

For our Economy Course we had to develop an app choosing a sector in which we could think it was worthy and useful. We came out with Aptive, which helps CEOs and venture capitalists of a company to monitor the stress of their employees.

Aladin

This is a project made for Lab of Computer Graphics. My group and I were supposed to graphically re-interpret a tale using 3ds max and so it was done. Do you like it?
